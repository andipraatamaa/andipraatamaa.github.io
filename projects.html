Automobile Company RFM, CLV And Churn Rate Analysiss
Background:

The company is a retail business specializing in the sale of automobiles and related services. With a diverse customer base, understanding customer behavior is critical for improving sales strategies and customer retention. Leveraging data analytics, the company aims to enhance its customer relationship management (CRM) efforts by identifying key customer segments, predicting customer lifetime value (CLV), and reducing churn rates.

Objective:

Segment Customers: Use RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) analysis to categorize customers into meaningful segments.

Predict Customer Lifetime Value (CLV): Estimate the future value of customers to prioritize marketing efforts.

Analyze Churn Rate: Identify the churn rate and the factors contributing to customer churn to develop effective retention strategies.

Method:

Exploratory Data Analysis(EDA), Recency Frequency and Monetary(RFM) Analysis, Customer lifetime Value(CLV) Analysis, Churn Rate Analysis

Result:

Our customer were 5 segments based on RFM.

Champions segment customers purchase frequently, spend the most, and have made recent purchases. They are highly engaged and valuable.

Loyal segment customers are regular buyers with moderate purchase frequency and monetary value. They show potential for long-term loyalty.

Potential segment customers have moderate purchase frequency and monetary value but might need more engagement to increase loyalty.

At Risk segment customers have not made purchases recently and have low purchase frequency and monetary value. They are at a high risk of churn.

Others segment is group includes varied characteristics with generally lower engagement and spend.

Approximately 12.36% of customers have churned, with churned customers showing higher recency (time since last purchase), lower frequency, and lower monetary value compared to active customers.

Customers with higher purchase frequency and value have a higher CLV. Prioritizing these customers can lead to better returns on marketing investments.
